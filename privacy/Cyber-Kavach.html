PRIVACY JUNCTION




In the recent times, the use of social media has increased massively.
And with that has increased the number of social media crimes. Social media crimes include people making threats, bullying, harassing, and stalking others online.
While much of this type of activity goes unpunished, or isn't taken seriously, victims of these types of crimes frequently don't know when to call the police.
So we have to ensure that we must be secure online.
Here are some tips to keep you safe on social media platforms like Facebook, Whatsapp, Instagram ,Twitter etc.



Protect your password

• Don't use your password anywhere else online, and never share it with other people.
• Your password should be hard to guess, so don't include your name or common words.
• Include some special characters like !, # etc. and numbers to make it stronger.
• Another way of strengthening your password is by using a passphrase, which is a password but like a sentence. For e.g., "Iamthecaptainofthesea1!". This makes it even more harder for hackers to guess it.


Secure the app with an app lock

• You must use a password to lock your app, so that anyone else can't use it.
• You can also use fingerprint for locking the app. For e.g., Whatsapp gives you a feature for fingerprint lock (see adjacent photo).
• Make sure to make it automatically lock after a specified time limit.

Lock your Profile

• You must lock your Profile on all social media apps.
• Locking your profile doesn't let anyone to take a screenshot or download your profile picture.
• It also ensures that only your friends and nobody else can see your posts and pictures.


Show less about you

• For chatting apps like Whatsapp, you should hide the 'last seen' timestamp.
• It may not seem like vital information, but if a scammer already knows some other things about you, adding that last piece of contextual information could prove useful to them.
• You can disable or restrict who sees your 'last seen' time in WhatsApp.


Some other tips

• You must never accept friend requests from unknown persons.
• NEVER click on links that look suspicious, even if they come from your friends or a person you know.
• Clicking on infected links may harm your device by injecting a virus or malware.
