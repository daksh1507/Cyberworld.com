1.
Cyber Peace Foundation
CPF

Among the very few organizations in the world working for ‘Peace’, the Cyber Peace Foundation stands to be the first NGO in the world to work for ‘Cyber Peace’. Cyber Peace Foundation focuses on awareness, counselling, education, training and reaching out to the citizens, the government firms, law enforcement agencies (LEAs), private enterprises, NGOs working in cyber-crime and cyber security, universities, cyber security experts and bug bounty hunters; to provide a common platform on a global level for all experts to come under a COMMON UMBRELLA. In its bid to curb the ever-increasing cybercrime menace and promote cyber harmony, Cyber Peace Foundation has a CYBER PEACE CORPS. We act as a point of contact for different governments, LEAs, and cyber cells to ensure peaceful settlement of any cyber related disputes. The aim of the organization is to empower through knowledge of threats, risks and opportunities. Cyber Peace Foundation acknowledges the importance of conservation of cyber ecosystem, in the same manner as we work to protect our real-world environment and is highly committed to this cause.

They have also launched eRaksha, the first competition in the world focusing exclusively on Digital Citizenship and Online Safety and tackling Fake News & Misinformation.

Learn More: https://www.cyberpeace.org/

3.
National Intelligence Grid
NATGRID

The National Intelligence Grid or NATGRID is the integrated intelligence master database structure for counter-terrorism purpose connecting databases of various core security agencies under Government of India collecting comprehensive patterns procured from 21 different organizations that can be readily accessed by security agencies round the clock.

The National Technical Research Organisation develops technology capabilities in aviation and remote sensing, data gathering and processing, cyber security, cryptology systems, strategic hardware and software development and strategic monitoring. It is a technical intelligence agency that acts under the National Security Advisor of India in the Prime Minister Office. It is India's prime Cyber Security Organisation.

5.
Cyber Swachhta Kendra
Botnet Cleaning and Malware Analysis Centre

To combat cyber security violations and prevent their increase, Government of India’s Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-in) in February 2017 launched ‘Cyber Swachhta Kendra’ (Botnet Cleaning and Malware Analysis Centre) a new desktop and mobile security solution for cyber security in India. It is a part of the Government of India's Digital India initiative under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) to create a secure cyber space by detecting botnet infections in India and to notify, enable cleaning and securing systems of end users so as to prevent further infections.

Learn More: https://cyberswachhtakendra.gov.in/
